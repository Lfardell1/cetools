All natural hazard claims with contents only (E.g. power surge to fridge) are small claims with two exceptions: EMB to building items & Glass only


This means that if the only damage is to the motor of a building item then it is small claims


All natural hazard claims that require a makesafe restoration due to water damaged must be created through a Do & Charge through claim center


This is because our natural hazard vendors are not normally equipped to deal with water damage unlike EOL


Natural hazard building claims are non-assessed or assessed and client managed


It's important to look out for these assessed triggers when lodging a claim


Assessed Triggers:

• Flood
• Hail damage to the main roof
• Mould present or damaged asbestos
• Hardwood Floors (not floating)
• Swimming pools, retaining walls and driveways
• Damage to 3+ rooms
• Extensive damage to the kitchen
• The whole level of cupboards water damaged
• Sewerage/Black/Grey Water damage
• Ceiling, Wall or Roof has collapsed
• Extensive cracking of internal walls/ceilings (not just paint)
• Suspected Ground Movement
• Significant damage to the roof of the house (impact, roof coming away, hail damaged roof, etc)
• Holes in a wall or roof where you can see the outside

Escape of Liquid claims must never be declined at lodgement based on a PDS exclusion


If the customer has a report directly stating that the claim confirms the claim is not covered we are to lodged as without prejudice


If the claim is accepted we will cover all of the neccessary and reasonable costs for exploratory work

If the claim is not accepted we still offer coverage for exploratory works but there is limits applicable. Check the relevent PDS for these limits


If there is carpets damage include the carpets as part of the building SOW


EOL claims will always be assessed and client managed even with just contents damage


Always be on the lookout for an advise the relevant exclusions for EOL and advise the customer as such


The most common ones are listed below:


• wear, tear and gradual deterioration or loss or damage as a result of a gradual process of liquid leaking, splashing, dripping or overflowing;

• loss or damage to, or caused by, a leaking shower floor or base, leaking shower cubicle walls, leaking shower glass screening or doors, leaking open shower floor areas or other wet areas •

EMB coverage depends on the particular policy


For our Suncorp and GIO policies the EMB coverage is optional so make sure to check if they have the coverage added to their policy

For these SUN and GIO policies the excess applicable is only $100

It's important to remember that under EMB cover we only cover the damaged motor under certain circumstances

Below is some example of when we do cover more than just the motor:

• the electric motor or compressor containing the motor;
• an entire sealed unit, filter, dryer and re-gassing if the electric motor is inside a sealed refrigeration or air conditioning unit;
• a swimming pool water pump, combined with its electric motor, if the replacement pump motor cannot be bought on its own.


EMB is coverage that can fit under different coverages sometimes. This is because we'll only cover the damaged motor.

For example if the item is damaged by storm damage (power surge) it would be more beneficial for the insured to have the claim lodged under natural hazards so we can cover the damage to the entire appliance instead of the just the motor


We'll never cover the installation for submersible pumps

Theft claims are nearly always small claims.


Normally small claims don't deal with building damage claims but the exception is with theft claims with minor building damage

If there is damage to more than one area or door we'll path the claim as assessed


Theft claims will begin with asking the insured for a list of stolen items + a police report and then EOO


Minor damage means things like a damaged front door / windows broken or locks damaged throughout the house


It's important to remember that we only cover the replacement of locks that have been damaged under theft claims even if the keys have been taken


The exception to this is that some policies will offer complete locks replacement up to a dollar limit if the keys are stolen


Normally in this case we'll ask the insured to provide an invoice for the lock replacement and we will reimburse


Most of the time make sure to offer a makesafe to the insured with theft claims as how to the assailant gained access to the property so they normally require us


An assessment will normally be triggered if any item taken is over $10,000 or if there is more than 10 items
This doesn't automatically mean we require an assessment. If the insured can provide a comprehensive list and EOO for items and their is no ambiguity in how the assailant entered the property or whether their is coverage based on the event.


An important thing to remember is that we only cover the insured for theft from their property. For strata / unit owners this means there is some instances in which the theft may not be covered as it was taken from a common area of their building (Which isn't considered their insured address) such as a garage.


This can be a grey area and it's important to check the relevant KM pages and with a HSO if you're ever unsure


When this does occur, look to cover the insured under other coverages if possible such as portable valuables or contents temporarly removed

Ensure we are checking for XS-free glass on the insured's policy


Without this a lot of the time the repairs may be uneconomical


In the case where this is ambigious lodge a claim and ask the insured to send in a personal quote for the repair before we proceed


Our policies also consider ceramic (sink / toilet cistern) as glass also


If the insured also has contents this extends to damaged glass furniture etc.


We always want to be sending jobs to our suppliers first. So for glass claims always aim to send the job to one of glaziers


Unless IO already has a quote/invoice in which case look to one touch the claim by verbally validating the details of the documents ($2000 limit)

Used for one touching claims when the insured has all the documents prepared for us.


Always ask the insured to send in documentation
